AI Newsroom

An agentic AI system that scouts, fact-checks and narrates a daily AI news podcast on its own. Built solo on the Claude Agent SDK. Runs free, on a schedule, no server.

6
Specialized agents

Claude Agent SDK Claude Haiku GitHub Actions Gemini TTS Python

Not one model call in a costume. A newsroom of six specialized agents that hand work to each other and argue before they publish. A Fact-checker can send the Writer's draft back for a second pass. That loop is what makes the output trustworthy enough to actually send.

The honest part: the agents were the easy twenty percent. Every real fight was in the glue. The free voice that worked on my Mac and got blocked from the cloud. The run that reported success and silently sent nothing. That is the real story of shipping AI.

About

Ishika Gupta

enterprise trained, startup tested

I am a product manager who builds. Five years shipping enterprise software, an MBA from Michigan State's Broad College of Business, and a habit of writing the code myself when an idea is worth proving. Plenty of PMs can describe an agentic AI system. I have built one that runs every morning without me.

I learned product at Siemens, inside a two billion dollar software portfolio. I took a failed feature from zero traction to 31% adoption by going back to customers and redesigning the roadmap around what they actually did, and I ran an Agile transformation that removed 15,000 engineering hours of rework a year. Big organizations taught me how decisions really get made, and how much patience it takes to move one.

The last two years I have spent at the front edge of AI, building. AI Newsroom is six agents on the Claude Agent SDK that scout the day's news, fact-check their own drafts and narrate a podcast, running free on a schedule with no server. I vibe-coded a live S&P 500 dashboard to find out what the new tools hand you and what they quietly leave on your desk. The answer, both times, was judgment.

The through-line has not changed since I wrote my first line of code. Find the real problem. Build the thing that solves it. Know when to stop. I am looking for a team building something hard, where a PM who can talk to engineers and executives in the same afternoon is exactly what the work needs.
