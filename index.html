AI Newsroom

An agentic AI system that scouts, fact-checks and narrates a daily AI news podcast on its own. Built solo on the Claude Agent SDK. Runs free, on a schedule, no server.

Not one model call in a costume. A newsroom of six specialized agents that hand work to each other and argue before they publish — with a Fact-checker that can send the Writer's draft back for a second pass. That loop is what makes the output trustworthy enough to actually send.

The honest part: the agents were the easy twenty percent. Every real fight was in the glue. The free voice that worked on my Mac and got blocked from the cloud. The run that reported success and silently sent nothing. That is the real story of shipping AI.

About

Product Manager Builder

enterprise trained, startup tested

I have my MBA from Michigan State's Broad College of Business. I came in from engineering — building systems, writing code, thinking in edge cases. The MBA gave me the vocabulary to take those instincts into bigger rooms: strategy, operations, leadership, supply chain. I graduated with a clearer picture of how large organizations make decisions, and a healthy impatience for how slowly they sometimes make them.

The last two years I've been at the edge of AI: shipping agentic systems, testing what vibe-coding actually teaches you about judgment, and figuring out what it means to build products when the tools themselves are still in flux.

The through-line has always been the same. Find the real problem. Build the thing that solves it. Know when to stop. That worked in code, and it works in product.

Outside work: long walks, books that make me think differently, and asking "why does this product exist" about everything I use.
